Group Formation

Rationale: Groups tend to go through similar stages, and being able to recognize and successfully navigate these stages is an important competency of a potential group member.

By the end of this lesson, the student will be able to:

  • Describe the 5 stages of the Tuckman Model of group formation

    1. The Tuckman Model of group formation

Body

  1. The Tuckman Model is a concise and easy-to-remember model of group development
    1. In the "Forming" stage, groups orient themselves to the group members.
    2. In the "Storming" stage, groups resolve the majority of conflict around group roles.
    3. In the "Norming" stage, groups establish their processes and their group culture.
    4. In the "Performing" stage, groups engage in their tasks at their highest level of efficiency.
    5. In the "Adjourning" stage, groups dissolve.
